Meal Sheer

This project was created during the UX design course by Google. The idea came from a social group that pet owner who loves cooking for their pets. As a pet owner myself, I also cook my dog’s meal every week, so I decided to work on this topic and take it further. The app provides the fresh meal recipe with nutrition information and an ingredient shopping list that allows the pet owner to explore healthy recipes for their little one.

What's the problem

Since pets have different nutritional needs than humans, fresh homemade meals consist of high-quality ingredient and dietary needs compared to processed food such as kibble. There are 87% of pet owners claimed that their pet’s eating regimen is extremely important to them. Pet owners had a hard time figuring out what’s good nutrition, ingredients, and sustainable food for their pets.

Solutions & Goals

Meal Sheer is a mobile app that helps pet owners find a healthy recipe meals for their pets. The recipe gives pet owners all the relevant information to make a confident decision before cooking a meal. It’s also a platform for pet parents to share their ideas, thoughts, and questions with community.

My role & process

I worked as the solo Product Designer for 1 months and have been responsible for the complete end-to-end UX process. Given the creative freedom to tackle this challenge, I refined my design framework to deeply understand the user. My approach to this project is to research, synthesize, ideate, design, and validate a suitable solution for the current problem space.

Responsibilities
Conducting interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs.

Test & Iterate

Since pets have different nutritional needs than humans, fresh homemade meals consist of high-quality ingredients and dietary needs compared to processed food such as kibble. There are 87% of pet owners claimed that their pet’s eating regimen is extremely important to them. According to the statistics, more than three-quarters of pet owners will only buy food that uses premium ingredients. Research shows that pet owners spend more time figuring out if pet food has good nutrition, high-quality ingredients, and sustainable quality.
Usability Study: Findings
I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframe to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype with improved screen design, some areas still need improvement and refining.
Round 1 Findings
01
Users want to view more categories on home page
02
Users want more filters on search
03
Users want to know what’s side effect when using bad ingredients
Round 2 Findings
01
When writing a recipe, should have a warming window of ingredients side effect and harmful ingredients should not be on the list.
02
Users want to add their own ingredients to the app
