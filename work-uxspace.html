What's the problem

Designers need a place where they can develop and implements their skills otherwise they’ll be left behind. The design world is built by creativity and it’s always shifting, changing, and growing day by day. As a newbie to the UX design world, I spent 3 months self-learning, taking a course online on UX design. Even though the course provided great study materials but with limited resources, designers like me had a hard time finding the expert’s help, expanding their knowledge, and networking with other designers. It has been highly challenging for self-learners to get more help in the field, mainly because we didn’t know how to start and how to build a solid design portfolio. I chose this topic because I want to help designers like me to be ready for the job market.

Solutions & Goals

With a website that offers design challenges practice and mentors for UX designers, they can quickly find the best match mentors and practice design problems in order to build up their design skills. The designer will be more confident and job-ready they will communicate and be mentored by experts in the field.
The goal of this program is to help junior UX designers grow their career fields.
The program focused on three objectives:
01
Matching
Matching junior UX designers with experienced professionals in the UX industry.
02
Practice
A place for designers to practice their design skills.
03
Conntected
Networking with others designers.

UX design course by Google provided good UX fundamentals, but in the end, they require students to build up a solid portfolio with 3 case studies.

Pain Points
  • It’s a challenge for people who don’t have much UX background
  • The course only provides peer reviews on the project, and most of the feedback is not useful and practical.
  • It’s hard to learn what a good UX case study and portfolio looks like on your own
  • Can’t afford expenses online Bootcamp (average cost around 10,000 to 15,000 USD for 3 months)
  • Limited access to feedback, and important critique and advice. And they use the same material over and over.

I interviewed 5 users, pet owners, who want to cook for their pet or have cooked for their pet in the past 2 years. Each interview was conducted remotely through video call and lasted about 10-30 minutes.

Test & Iterate

Since pets have different nutritional needs than humans, fresh homemade meals consist of high-quality ingredients and dietary needs compared to processed food such as kibble. There are 87% of pet owners claimed that their pet’s eating regimen is extremely important to them. According to the statistics, more than three-quarters of pet owners will only buy food that uses premium ingredients. Research shows that pet owners spend more time figuring out if pet food has good nutrition, high-quality ingredients, and sustainable quality.
Usability Study: Findings
I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframe to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype with improved screen design, some areas still need improvement and refining.
Round 1 Findings
01
Users want to view more categories on home page
02
Users want more filters on search
03
Users want to know what’s side effect when using bad ingredients
Round 2 Findings
01
When writing a recipe, should have a warming window of ingredients side effect and harmful ingredients should not be on the list.
02
Users want to add their own ingredients to the app
