Project vision

Designers need a place where they can develop and implements their skills otherwise they’ll be left behind. The design world is built by creativity and it’s always shifting, changing, and growing day by day. As a newbie to the UX design world, I spent 3 months self-learning, taking a course online on UX design. Even though the course provided great study materials but with limited resources, designers like me had a hard time finding the expert’s help, expanding their knowledge, and networking with other designers. It has been highly challenging for self-learners to get more help in the field, mainly because we didn’t know how to start and how to build a solid design portfolio. I chose this topic because I want to help designers like me to be ready for the job market.

Problem

How might I design a platform that can help junior designers move forward and get valuable advice on industry practice?

Solution

With a website that offers design challenges practice and mentors for UX designers, they can quickly find the best match mentors and practice design problems in order to build up their design skills. The designer will be more confident and job-ready they will communicate and be mentored by experts in the field.

Meet the users

Based on user research and secondary research, I created two personas. Each persona highlights their concerns, goals, and current stage when pursuing a new career in UX design. The persona referred to users throughout the entire product development process to make sure I am on the right track and define users’ pain points in my design decisions.
